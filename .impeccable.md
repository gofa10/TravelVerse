## Design Context

### Users
TravelVerse is a multi-surface travel intermediary platform with three distinct surfaces:

- Customer-facing booking flows are the primary product surface and should be optimized first for end travelers discovering and booking experiences.
- Tour Guide profile pages are trust-critical conversion surfaces and should turn skepticism into purchase confidence through clarity, warmth, and credibility.
- Admin interfaces are utility-first operational tools where speed, legibility, and task completion matter more than expressive aesthetics.

The core job to be done is helping travelers move from browsing to confident booking without the friction, clutter, or fatigue common in overloaded travel marketplaces.

### Brand Personality
The brand personality is: Trusted, Warm, Effortless.

TravelVerse should feel human, editorial, and considered rather than machine-generated or overly commercial. Interfaces should create confidence without pressure, personality without noise, and polish without sterility. The emotional goal is calm confidence: users should feel guided, reassured, and subtly inspired to continue exploring and booking.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction should avoid the look of cluttered OTA aggregators, cold enterprise dashboards, and generic AI-generated travel UI. The design should favor a warm, slightly desaturated palette with light mode as the primary expression.

References implied by the direction:

- Warm-leaning, slightly muted primary blue rather than electric saturated blue.
- Warm terracotta or dusty gold CTA accents rather than standard amber or orange.
- Warm off-whites and beige-tinted neutrals instead of cold grey-first surfaces.
- Deep warm-tinted dark neutrals for dark mode instead of pure black or default slate.

Typography should prefer Plus Jakarta Sans or DM Sans over default-feeling stacks such as Inter or Poppins unless there is a strong product reason to keep them. Hierarchy should be clearly differentiated across hero copy, section headers, body content, and metadata labels.

Component styling should feel intentionally varied rather than mechanically uniform:

- Inputs should stay tighter, around 6px radius.
- Cards should feel more generous, around 16px radius.
- Pill badges should use fully rounded treatment only where semantically appropriate.
- Shadows should be applied by surface type, not reused indiscriminately.
- Gradients should favor centered radial treatments or diagonal 135 degree movement, avoiding generic default gradient patterns.
- Iconography should use a single family such as Lucide or Heroicons with a consistent stroke weight and rounded caps where possible.

Existing token support in `frontend/src/index.css` should be extended incrementally, not replaced with a full design system rewrite.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize traveler confidence first. Customer booking surfaces and trust-critical guide profiles should always get the strongest visual and UX attention.
- Make warmth legible through restraint. Use warm neutrals, muted contrast, and selective accent color rather than loud palettes or aggressive merchandising patterns.
- Prefer intentional variation over system monotony. Radius, spacing, shadow, and composition should adapt to component purpose instead of feeling mechanically identical.
- Keep travel content visually dominant. Light mode is the primary canvas, and destination imagery, cards, and editorial sections should breathe on warm bright surfaces.
- Build forward through tokens. Improve the current CSS variable system incrementally so light and dark mode remain coherent without a rewrite.
- Maintain accessibility as a baseline, not a pass later. All color choices should meet WCAG AA contrast, and all motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Design like a human traveler made it. Every screen should feel considered, grounded, and specific rather than generated from generic travel-product tropes.
